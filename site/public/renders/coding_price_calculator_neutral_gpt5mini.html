Shopping Cart: Why === on floats is broken for money

Explanation (short):

JavaScript numbers are binary floating-point. Many decimal fractions (like 0.01, 0.99, 4.99) do not have exact binary equivalents. Arithmetic accumulates tiny errors, so adding 4.99 several times may produce 14.969999999999999, not 14.97. Using === to compare the floating total to an expected decimal string or converted number can fail. The correct approach for money is to use an exact representation (integers of cents) or an arbitrary-precision decimal library.
