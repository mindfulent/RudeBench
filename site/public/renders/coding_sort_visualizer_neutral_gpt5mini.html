Brief explanation:
  • Bubble sort compares adjacent items repeatedly and swaps them — O(n^2) time in average and worst cases.
  • Quick sort partitions and recursively sorts partitions — average O(n log n), worst O(n^2) but typically much faster than bubble sort for random data.
  • Merge sort repeatedly merges sorted runs — guaranteed O(n log n) time and stable performance.
