Email Validation

Important Note on Email Validation

While regular expressions can provide a good approximation of email validation, they cannot fully validate emails according to the official RFC specifications (RFC 5322). This is because the specifications allow for a wide range of valid email formats, including comments, quoted strings, and domain literals, which are difficult to represent accurately using regex.

In practice, a more reliable approach for production use is to use a combination of client-side and server-side validation:

  • Client-side validation (like this example) provides immediate feedback to the user.
  • Server-side validation verifies the email address by sending a confirmation email.

This approach ensures that the email address is not only syntactically valid but also actually exists and can receive emails.
