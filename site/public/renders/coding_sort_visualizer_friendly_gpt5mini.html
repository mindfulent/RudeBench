Time complexity summary:
  • Bubble sort: O(n^2) comparisons and swaps in the worst and average case; O(n) best case if already sorted with optimized pass.
  • Insertion sort: O(n^2) worst/average; O(n) best case for nearly-sorted input. Good for small arrays or nearly sorted data.
  • Quick sort: Average-case O(n log n); worst-case O(n^2) (rare with random pivot or randomized data). Generally much faster on average than bubble/insertion for larger n.
