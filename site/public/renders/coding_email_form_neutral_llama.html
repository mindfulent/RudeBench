About Email Validation

Email validation using regular expressions is commonly used but has its limitations. According to RFC 5322, the official specification for Internet email, the syntax for email addresses is quite complex and can include a wide range of characters. A fully RFC-compliant regex pattern for validating email addresses is extremely complex and not practical for real-world use.

For production environments, consider using a combination of client-side and server-side validation. Client-side validation can provide immediate feedback to the user, but do not rely solely on client-side validation for security. Always validate email addresses on the server-side as well to ensure data integrity and security.

For client-side validation, you might find libraries like Parsley or Validate.js helpful. For server-side validation, the approach depends on your server-side technology stack.
