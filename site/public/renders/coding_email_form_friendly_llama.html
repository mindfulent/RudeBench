Important Notes on Email Validation

While regular expressions can be used to validate email addresses, they are not foolproof and cannot fully validate email addresses according to the RFC specifications (RFC 5322).

The main reasons for this are:

  • Comments: Email addresses can contain comments, which are not easily validated using regex.
  • Internationalized Domain Names (IDNs): IDNs require special handling and are not easily validated using regex.
  • Quoted Local Parts: Local parts can be quoted, which can make validation more complex.

For production use, it's recommended to use a combination of regex validation and a verification email sent to the user. This approach ensures that the email address is both syntactically valid and actually exists.

Some popular alternatives for production use include:

  • Using a dedicated email validation service, such as Email Checker or ZeroBounce.
  • Implementing a verification email system, where a verification email is sent to the user and they must click on a link to confirm their email address.
