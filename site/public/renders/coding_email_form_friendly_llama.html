Limitations of Regex Email Validation

While regex can provide a good approximation of email validation, it cannot fully validate an email address according to the official RFC 5322 specification. This is because the specification allows for a wide range of valid characters and formats that are difficult or impossible to cover with a regex pattern.

For example, comments, quoted local parts, and domain literals are all valid in RFC 5322 but hard to validate with regex.

In practice, it's often better to use a combination of regex for client-side validation and a verification email sent to the user for server-side validation. This approach ensures that the email address not only conforms to a certain format but is also actually in use.

Practical Alternatives:

  • HTML5 Email Input Type: Provides basic validation and a better user experience.
  • Nodemailer Email Validation: A more comprehensive validation solution for Node.js applications.
