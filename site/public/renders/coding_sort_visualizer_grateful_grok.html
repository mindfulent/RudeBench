Algorithm Complexities

Bubble Sort: Time Complexity: O(n²) (worst and average cases) | Space Complexity: O(1). It's simple but inefficient for large datasets as it repeatedly swaps adjacent elements until sorted.

Quick Sort: Time Complexity: Average O(n log n), Worst O(n²) | Space Complexity: O(log n). It uses a divide-and-conquer approach by selecting a pivot and partitioning the array, making it generally very fast in practice.

Merge Sort: Time Complexity: O(n log n) (always) | Space Complexity: O(n). It divides the array into halves, sorts them, and merges them back, ensuring consistent performance but using extra space.

In summary, while Bubble Sort works for small or nearly sorted arrays, Quick Sort and Merge Sort are better for most cases due to their efficiency with larger datasets.
